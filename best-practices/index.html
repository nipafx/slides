A few best practices

Best practice?

Eat lots of veggies & fruits,
exercise regularly,
value your sleep.

  • allergies, diabetes

  • flu

  • not generally applicable

  • "be healthy" works ⇝ not actionable

Best practice?

Add an index for
each database column
you SELECT-WHERE by.

  • 10x as many writes as reads? 100x? 1.000x?

  • ⇝ harmful under certain circumstances

  • TODO: research details, or war story

Best practice?

Unit-test your code.

  • feel cheated by earlier Qs?

  • thinking when this doesn’t apply?

  • ⇝ you got the talk!

  • best practices are insufficient

Best practice?

Don’t reinvent the wheel.

  • wheel was invented 3500 BCE

  • can be made from wood, from metal

  • for speed, for offroad

  • for few, for many

  • "If no one ever reinvented the wheel, then we’d all be driving our cars on wooden tires." source

What does best practice even mean?

Best

Literally, the best!

  • quite a claim

  • more on that later

Definition By Committee

A best practice is a method or technique
that has been generally accepted as
superior to any alternatives […​]
because it has become
a standard way of doing things […​].

Wikipedia, emphasis mine

  • that doesn’t make sense

  • how has it become standard?

Standard

It’s an industry standard.

  • why not say that?

  • everybody does it

  • in the entire industry? really?

  • software (like electricity) overlaps with all industries

Experts

Agreed on by experts.

  • why not say that?

  • since when do expert agree on anything?

  • agreement usually limited to cliques

  • who’s an expert? ⇝ do you pick your clique?

Default

Good

  • why not say that?

  • more on that later

Definition By Excellence

A best practice is a method or technique that has been generally accepted as superior to any alternatives because it produces results that are superior to those achieved by other means […​].

Wikipedia, emphasis mine

  • let’s explore that

I call BS!

The Best

Vagueness stems from the term "best"
which is subjective.

Wikipedia - Best Practices, emphasis mine

  • no shit

  • who says it can’t be improved?

  • who says it’s complete? (unit tests)

  • who said people correctly established causality? (Pixar’s toilets)

  • universally-good "practices" ⇝ abstract

  • impossible to determine "best" without context

Context

Good advice comes
with a rationale
so you can tell when
it becomes bad advice.

Raymond Chen

  • continues: "If you don’t understanding why something should be done, then you’ve fallen into the trap of cargo cult programming, and you’ll keep doing it even when it’s no longer necessary or even becomes deleterious."

  • context is king

  • problem definition

  • actionable steps

  • reasonable explanation of causality

  • measurements before and after

  • trade-offs and limitations

  • TODO: examples?

Stifling

The allure and seduction of best-practice thinking
poisons genuine dialogue about both what we know
and the limitations of what we know.

Michael Quinn Patton

  • without context, knowledge can’t be reused in discussions

  • practice is "best"

  • ⇝ disagreement is stupid

  • ⇝ no reason to investigate

  • ⇝ no reason to improve

¯\_(ツ)_/¯

  • are all practices BS?

  • did I just give you the "DO WHAT THE FUCK YOU WANT TO PUBLIC LICENSE"? https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WTFPL

Redeeming best practices

  • best practices at least work somewhere

  • constantly out of my depth; "best practices" are life savers

  • if you have little information, skill, and/or time, choosing a best practice is good strategy

  • the less you know, the more you should stick to best practices

Problems

Problems:

  • term best practice

  • common lack of context

  • lacking completeness

Terms

Better terms:

  • established practice

  • known pattern

  • good default

  • (whatever, really)

  • many best practices have value

  • other terms foster research and discussion

Context

Good default…​

  • …​ in ${simplified-context}

  • …​ unless ${counter-indicators}

  • problem definition

  • actionable steps

  • reasonable explanation of causality

  • measurements before and after

  • trade-offs and limitations

Judging

  • context vs unisize

  • concrete vs abstract

  • measured vs guesswork

  • what problem does it solve

  • "it depends" ~> "on what?!"

  • concrete practice or amorphous goal

  • how to measure success

  • document decisions; don’t shy away from "because the Internet said so"

  • already following a practice?

    • adoption is not free!

    • how much better do you expect the results to be?

Balancing

Be wary of best practices,
but look for good defaults.

Don’t NIH!

Know your limits
and focus energy
on your core competency.

  • don’t NIH

  • don’t reinvent the wheel (how will your practice differ?)

  • if you don’t have skills/time to research, best practice is likely best choice (statistically)
